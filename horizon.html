UI/UX Case Study

horizon news

Tech news curation and control. Create your own categories, follow your favorite writers and have the news come to you.

The objective of this project was to design and prototype a user interface that would allow consumers to curate their own tech news feed. Process included market research, persona creation, wireframing, comping and prototyping.

user personas

Caleb is millennial who always buys the latest gadgets. He’s a loyal Apple consumer and Tesla driver. Recently he’s become much more interested in the business side of the tech industry for investment reasons. He doesn’t subscribe to any news services and gets the majority of his news from social media.

Gary is a software engineer by trade and has invested a lot of his personal income into “smart home” and green technology. He loves staying up to date on the practical applications of tech in everyday life. As the father of 4 and breadwinner of the family, Gary has little time to seek out news in his niche field of interest. Despite being subscribed to both the NYT and WSJ digital plans, he still has to hunt for what interests him. He finds that most mainstream news platforms only comment on tech through a political lens.

wireframes

library

The Library is the central hub. This is where the user can browse or create their own categories.

The list of user-created categories became known as "the gutter" when designing.

From the Library, users should be able to continue reading their most recently viewed article.

discover

Based off a user's categories and bookmarks, the Discover page is a collection of curated articles.

The title of the article, the author, how many minutes it takes to read and the ability to quickly bookmark for later are all present on each card.

Card designs let us display as much relevant information as possible for the user before they decide to click.

profile

The profile page acts as the notification hub for the user.

The profile page also acts as a history page -- displaying a user's most recent reading.

A highlighted ring around a writer acts as a quick way to see if they've published something new.
